Airbus History

The range of civil aircraft ranges from 107 to 525 seats and includes the A320 single-lane family (A318, A319, A320 and A321), the A330/A340/A350 wide-body long-haul aircraft, and the A380 double-deck very long-haul aircraft. The A300, A310 and A340 are no longer produced but are still in service. The A320, A330 and A380 are in production and the A350 entered service in 2015.

The first aircraft manufactured by Airbus, the A300B, was named because it was originally intended to carry 300 people as standard. Thereafter, each new model had a name increasing by 10 in 10: A310, A320, A330, A340, the shortened or lengthened versions of the A320 receiving a very close number (A318, A319, A321).

The name of the last aircraft produced by Airbus, the A380, was chosen to recall the two rows of superimposed windows, but also because the 8 is a lucky figure in Asia, which represents the main target market for this aircraft. The A350 type follows the usual nomenclature.
